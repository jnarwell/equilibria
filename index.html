EQUILIBRIA

cyberdeck  ×  solarpunk  ×  thermodynamic equilibrium

Four machines, each held in dynamic balance. Not pictures of equilibrium — systems that are equilibrium, running forever, conserving what they spend.

The premise is a coalition — technology, capital, politics, welfare — bound to one task: the continuous rebalancing of society through developed abundance and return. Energy dissipated is recaptured. Material discarded is returned. Solar, distributed manufacture, closed loops. Not a utopia — a framework for moving past need.

The aesthetic is the argument. Amber is dissipation, heat, the cyberdeck's salvaged glow. Verdigris is capture, life, the solarpunk return. Every piece runs that gradient as a metabolism: born hot, cooled, recaptured, reborn. The terminal annotates the garden. The idea is the machine that makes the art.
